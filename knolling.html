Knolling & Poster Design.

knoll· ing (verb): "the process of arranging like objects in parallel or 90 degree angles as a method of organization."

The task was to create a poster that represented the basics of poster design within a poster. It needed to be self explanatory. Some critique I have of my own work is based on the fact that the text says "poster design." This would be stronger if it was a call to action (such as "just start..."). One problem I found is that people are always scared to take the first step; they do not want to make fresh marks on a clean sheet of paper for fear they might fail. This poster tries to relieve that fear because the first marks are already there.

This project was completed as part of PDI Studio Three in Fall 2013. I collaborated with Evan Fouché, Jeff Nassar, and David Rosenberg.
