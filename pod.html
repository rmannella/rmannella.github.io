About the Pods

Progressing from a modular wall with many cells to individual pods, this lighting project is for functional work and play spaces. We explored many forms and settled on two: one provides seated table space similar to a picnic table, while the other is lounging space. The designs are ergonomic and use a combination of wood and metal to create an inviting atmosphere. As a result of creating seperate pods, our design can be organized into any arrangement allowing either a single pod or multiple pods to be placed at certain locations on campus. Solar panels within the planks generate and store energy, which provide lighting in the pods at night.

Material Choices & Details

FRAME: The frame is two-parts and is manufactured from plasma-cut, galvanized steel. It is fixed to the ground by supports.

SOLAR PANELS The solar panels are incredibly thin and sit on top of the planks. They are connected to the LEDs via a wire and battery system that runs within the plank.

PLANK The planks are made from Heveatech, a long-lasting wood produced specifically for outdoor uses. It has a life span of approximately 25-30 years. The planks are fastened to the frame by L-brackets.

PLANK DETAILS The planks are manufactured to support solar panels and to house the battery, wiring, and LEDs that provide lighting at night. The plank is assembled by brackets fastened between the frames.
